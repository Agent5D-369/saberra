### POST 2 — Behind the Build (Post Wed or Thu)

We almost built a better meeting notetaker.

I am glad we didn't.

Here's the problem with meeting notetakers:

They produce transcripts. Sometimes summaries. Occasionally action items that nobody looks at two weeks later.

But a decision made in a meeting is not the same as a decision that becomes durable organizational memory.

To get from one to the other, someone has to:

→ Recognize the decision was made
→ Extract it cleanly
→ File it where the organization can find it
→ Make sure the right people know it exists
→ Keep it updated as circumstances change

That is 4–5 steps that require judgment.

AI does not have organizational judgment.

Humans do.

So we built something different: a system where AI extracts the decision candidates, and a human approves what actually becomes trusted memory.

The AI does the boring parts.
The human does the important part.
The organization gets memory it can actually trust.

Three months of building before we felt good about that distinction.

Would your team trust memory that no human reviewed?
