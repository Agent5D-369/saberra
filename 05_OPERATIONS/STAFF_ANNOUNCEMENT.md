Hi everyone,

I'm excited to share something we've been building for the past few weeks: **Living Memory**, Amora's shared institutional memory system.

## What it does

Living Memory automatically captures important meetings and emails and organizes them into shared, searchable memory in Notion — so that what we discuss, decide, and commit to doesn't live only in someone's inbox or personal notes.

The system captures:
- **Meeting recordings, transcripts, and notes** from Google Meet
- **Important emails** that are CCed, BCCed, or forwarded to a shared capture address

From this material, it extracts and stores:
- Meeting summaries and key discussion points
- Tasks with owners and due dates
- Decisions (classified as Confirmed, Candidate, or Needs Clarification)
- Risks with severity and mitigation notes
- Governance signals — tensions, proposals, role updates, policy changes

Everything goes into Notion as a **draft or candidate record**. Nothing becomes official or "canon" without a human decision. Automation handles intake. We handle authority.

## How to participate — it's simple

**For meetings:**
1. Schedule your meeting as normal
2. Add `roots@amora.cr` as a guest
3. Turn on recording and/or transcript when appropriate
4. Name decisions, owners, and next steps clearly during the meeting
5. After the meeting, the system will process the transcript or notes automatically

**For emails:**
- **CC** `roots@amora.cr` when an email should visibly enter project memory
- **BCC** `roots@amora.cr` when capture is appropriate but you don't want to add another visible recipient
- **Forward** important existing threads to `roots@amora.cr`
- Add `[AMORA CAPTURE]` to the subject line if it helps

**You never need to log into roots@amora.cr. Just CC, BCC, or forward — that's it.**

## What does NOT need to be captured

Please use judgment. Not everything needs to go into Living Memory.

Don't capture:
- Casual check-ins and scheduling logistics
- Private emotional processing or personal conversations
- Early brainstorming with no outcomes
- Sensitive sharing without consent

If something sensitive ends up in the system, it is automatically routed to a restricted review queue that only designated stewards can access — it does not become general memory.

## How to use Living Memory

Once records are in Notion, you can:
- Browse the Notion databases directly for meetings, tasks, decisions, and risks
- Use the **Amora Living Memory Hub** project in Claude Team to ask questions in plain language

Examples of questions you can ask Claude Team:
- "What tasks came out of the Q2 planning meeting?"
- "What decisions have we made about the land stewardship agreement?"
- "Who is the Circle Lead for Operations?"
- "What risks are currently flagged as high severity?"
- "What memory candidates are waiting for review?"

To use Claude Team, open the **Amora Living Memory Hub** project and start a conversation. You can paste Notion records or summaries directly into the chat.

## What this is not

Living Memory is not surveillance. It is shared remembrance and coordination.

The system does not read your personal email, access files you haven't shared with roots@amora.cr, or make any decisions on its own. It creates drafts and candidates. Stewards and admins review and approve anything that affects governance, policy, or official institutional memory.
