## PART 2: THE PRODUCT (PLAIN ENGLISH)

### What Saberra Does in One Sentence
Saberra watches your organization's inbox, captures everything from your Google Meet meetings and important emails, extracts every decision, task, risk, and insight with AI, files it all into structured Notion databases, and lets you ask Sera — your AI Chief of Staff — any question about your organization's history.

### The Zero-Behavior-Change Principle
This is the most important product truth and must be central to all marketing.

> **Your team changes nothing. Zero new tools to log into. Zero new forms to fill. Zero new habits to build.**

Here is the complete flow:
1. Your team has a Google Meet meeting (they're already doing this)
2. Meeting ends. Google automatically sends emails: recording link, transcript, AI notes (already happening)
3. Saberra watches a dedicated inbox. It sees those emails. (Invisible to your team)
4. Saberra reads the transcript with Claude AI. It extracts every decision, task, risk, person mentioned, and knowledge worth keeping. (Still invisible)
5. All extracted items land in your Notion workspace as draft records — ready for human review. (First visible moment)
6. An admin spends 20 minutes reviewing the queue. Approves the real ones.
7. That knowledge is now in Sera's memory — forever.
8. Six months later, someone asks: "What did we decide about the vendor contract in April?" Sera answers in 10 seconds with source links.

### What Sera Can Answer
Sera answers from your organization's actual documented history — not the internet, not guesswork:

- "What did we decide about the vendor contract in April?"
- "Who is currently responsible for the onboarding process?"
- "What are all the open risks related to our technology infrastructure?"
- "Summarize everything that happened in last month's governance meeting."
- "What tasks were assigned to Maria that are still open?"
- "Has anyone proposed changes to the conflict resolution policy?"
- "What were the key decisions from Q1 board meetings?"
- "Who is our point of contact at [organization name]?"

### What Saberra Captures Automatically
From every meeting and email processed, Saberra extracts twelve categories of structured information:

1. Meeting summaries (one-sentence + detailed)
2. People and organizations (profiles, relationships, roles)
3. Tasks and action items (with assignee, due date, priority)
4. Decisions (with confidence level and full context)
5. Risks (with severity and mitigation notes)
6. Knowledge worth preserving (facts, practices, lessons learned)
7. Projects and initiatives (with status and team associations)
8. Team circles or departments
9. Defined roles (who holds what, since when)
10. Role assignments (human to role mapping, with terms)
11. Governance proposals (potential policy or structure changes)
12. Formal governance actions (ledger entries for structured organizations)

### Critical Trust Feature: Human Review First
**Nothing Saberra extracts goes directly into permanent record.** Every item lands as draft/candidate/pending. A human reviews and approves before it becomes institutional memory. This is a feature, not a limitation — it makes every organization's Sera trustworthy because the memory is human-curated, not robot-assumed.

### Data Privacy and Ownership
100% of all data lives in the client's own Notion workspace and their own Railway hosting instance. Saberra never stores client data on its own servers. Clients own their memory.
