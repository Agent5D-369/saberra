# The Diagnostic Share Loop: A Canon for Organic Growth

This document serves as the canonical reference for the "Diagnostic Share Loop," an organic growth tactic that transforms passive lead magnets into viral, relational tools. Rooted in human psychology—recognition, validation, and shared clarity—this loop leverages moments of insight to spread through teams, ecosystems, and organizations. It shifts from promotional sharing ("Subscribe to this") to diagnostic sharing ("Is this us?"), recruiting clusters of stakeholders rather than isolated individuals.

Use this as the foundational framework for sales materials, including presentations, landing pages, articles, posts, and funnels. Frame it as a 2026 growth hack: In an era of information overload, people spread diagnostics that name unspoken truths, not generic content. Emphasize its efficiency (one lead multiplies without paid traffic), trust-building (identity-driven invites), and scalability (organizational flywheel). Back it with expert alignments (Hormozi, Myron Golden) and real-world examples to drive engagement.

## Core Concept: From Promotion to Recognition

Traditional sharing is weak: People do not forward newsletters, founders, or Substacks. Instead, they share epiphanies like "This named exactly what's happening in our project," "This is the thing I’ve been trying to explain," or "Read this and tell me if this is us." The Diagnostic Share Loop captures this by turning a lead magnet (e.g., a report) into a tool for collective diagnosis, spreading through founder ecosystems, DAOs, nonprofits, communities, and regenerative organizations.

**Why It Works in 2026:** Attention is scarce; clarity is currency. This loop makes sharing feel perceptive, confusion-reducing, and conversation-starting—motivators that align with how people naturally spread ideas.

## How the Diagnostic Share Loop Works

The loop is a 5-step process that activates after someone opts in:

1. **Opt-In:** Person accesses the report or diagnostic tool.
2. **Recognition:** They see their likely dominant pattern, triggering a "this is us" moment.
3. **Validation Need:** Sudden insight creates a desire for confirmation.
4. **Share Action:** They forward to co-founders, operators, board members, team leads, or friends, asking "Is this our pattern?"
5. **Cluster Formation:** Recipients engage, adopting the shared language, turning one lead into a group.

This is non-promotional: It recruits stakeholders into a common framework, building clusters instead of solo subscribers.

## Simplest Implementation

Start without complex software. Focus on three elements:

1. **Highly Shareable Result Frame:** End the report with a vivid diagnosis (e.g., "Your dominant pattern: F3 Governance Distortion—Power and decisions drifting out of alignment, tightening the project from inside"). Add a trigger: "Forward this to the co-founder or team member who's been trying to name the same problem."
2. **Co-Diagnosis Invitation:** Include: "Compare your read. Send to someone else and ask: 'Which pattern do you think is most active in our project right now?'" This turns it relational.
3. **Shared Language Asset:** Position a follow-up resource (e.g., Navigation Legend) as a team tool: "Use this to compare patterns, pillars, and failures together." It evolves from individual to collaborative.

## Optimal Funnel Integration

Embed the loop in your architecture for maximum spread:

- **Landing Page (e.g., StopTheCollapse.com):** Offer the "Top 7 Collapse Patterns Report" as the entry.
- **Thank-You/Report Delivery Page:** Display results with prompts like "Your next step: Identify your dominant pattern," "Forward to someone in the same project," and "Compare results."
- **Welcome Email:** Ask "Which pattern feels most active?" Then: "If someone else needs to see this, forward the report."
- **Email 2 (Navigation Legend Delivery):** Frame as "The shared map for people inside the same project"—a multi-person alignment tool.
- **Deeper Funnel:** Audiobook and Builder Pass for depth after team alignment.

This creates a real funnel: Report for first click, diagnosis for recognition, loop for spread, Legend for alignment, premium offers for commitment.

## The Viral Mechanic

People share what:

- Makes them look perceptive.
- Reduces confusion.
- Starts hard conversations.
- Names what everyone feels but cannot articulate.

Your framework hits all four, shifting growth from audience-building to organizational penetration: One share sparks chains in group chats, boards, and teams.

## Exact Phrases to Trigger Sharing

Use these lines to ignite the loop:

- "This pattern is easier to repair when more than one person can see it. Forward to the person carrying the same project with you. Ask: 'Which pattern do you think is most active right now?'"
- "The fastest way to test if this is real: Compare your diagnosis with someone else in the system."
- "Do not keep this diagnostic to yourself if more than one person is carrying the same project. Forward and compare your read."

Tie asks to diagnosis, team reality, recognition, and tough talks—not generic promotion.

## What Not to Do

Avoid weak, promotional language:

- "Share this with friends."
- "Tell others about this."
- "Help spread the word."

Targets seek clarity, not hype. Generic asks fail; diagnostic ones thrive.

## Highest-Leverage Upgrade

Add a post-report CTA: "Want the team version? Get the Navigation Legend to compare patterns across your project." This positions the Legend as a collaborative essential, boosting value and retention.
