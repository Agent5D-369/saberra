# 6. Messaging framework

## Category definition

# **Institutional Memory Infrastructure**

A system that captures, structures, reviews, and retrieves the living history of an organization.

## Hero statement

**Turn every meeting, decision, task, and risk into searchable institutional memory without changing how your team works.**

## Core value pillars

### 1. Capture without behavior change

Your team keeps using Google Meet. The system captures transcripts and notes automatically through a monitored inbox, processes them with Claude, and sends structured records into Notion for review.

### 2. Remember across time, not just one meeting

Fireflies, Otter, and Read AI summarize meetings. Living Memory Hub builds cumulative memory across meetings, roles, risks, policies, and decisions. Fireflies pricing starts around $10/user/month annually for Pro and $19/user/month annually for Business, while Otter runs around $16.99/user/month monthly for Pro and $30/user/month for Business.

### 3. Human-reviewed memory, not blind automation

The AI proposes. Humans approve. That is a crucial trust layer for decisions, policies, governance, and risk records.

## Objection responses

**“We already use Fireflies/Otter/Read AI.”**

Those tools capture meetings. This captures organizational memory. The difference is whether your team gets a meeting summary or a searchable operational record across months and years.

**“Why not just use Notion AI?”**

Notion AI helps with what is already in Notion. Living Memory Hub captures what your team forgets to put there.

**“Setup sounds complicated.”**

Yes. That is why this is sold as done-for-you. The client does not self-configure Google OAuth, Notion schemas, Railway, API keys, or IMAP. The implementation package exists to absorb that complexity.

**“Can we trust the AI?”**

You do not have to blindly trust it. The system routes extracted items into review workflows before they become durable memory.

**“Is this secure?”**

The strongest answer is data ownership: records live in the client’s Notion workspace and cloud infrastructure, not in a proprietary black-box database. The technical stack still needs a formal security page before selling to larger clients.
