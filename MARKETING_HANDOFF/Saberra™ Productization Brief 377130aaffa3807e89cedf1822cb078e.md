# Saberra™ Productization Brief

> **Institutional memory for teams that can’t afford to forget.**
> 
> 
> Saberra turns meetings, emails, decisions, tasks, risks, and governance records into structured, queryable organizational memory.
> 

---

## 1. The Gap Saberra Fills

Most tools fall into one of two buckets:

Every competitor does one of two things - they either require humans to manually write knowledge in (Notion AI, Guru, Confluence), or they produce ephemeral meeting summaries that go nowhere (Fathom, Fireflies, Otter). 

Nobody closes the loop automatically. Saberra is the only tool that listens passively and writes structured, queryable institutional memory with zero manual input.

| Category | Examples | Core Problem |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Manual knowledge bases** | Notion AI, Guru, Confluence | Humans still have to write, update, and organize the knowledge. |
| **Meeting summary tools** | Fathom, Fireflies, Otter | They create summaries, but those summaries usually don’t become structured institutional memory. |

### Saberra closes the loop.

Saberra listens passively, captures what matters, and writes structured, queryable institutional memory with **zero manual input from the team**.

It does not just summarize what happened.

It remembers.

## 2. Competitive Opening

The closest funded competitor appears to be **Interloom**, which raised **$16.5M in March 2026** to solve “tacit knowledge for AI agents” through enterprise context graphs.

Saberra’s advantage is different:

| Interloom | Saberra |

| Context graphs for AI agents | Institutional memory for real teams |

### The window is open.

Saberra has a working product today, a clear wedge, and a sharper path into underserved teams that need memory infrastructure before they are ready for enterprise AI platforms.

# 3. Sharpest ICP: Start Here

## Primary ICP

# Self-Managed Organizations

**Examples:** Holacracy, Sociocracy, Teal, distributed governance, worker-owned, cooperative, regenerative, purpose-driven, and self-managing teams.

### Why this ICP is the strongest starting point

Saberra’s data model already speaks their native language:

- Circles
- Roles
- Role Assignments
- Decision Candidates
- Governance Ledger
- Distributed decision-making
- Human-reviewed canon
- Organizational memory

No other tool is clearly built for this.

Holaspirit is the closest adjacent competitor, but it is primarily a role-mapping and governance platform, not an automatic memory capture system.

### Why they feel the pain intensely

Self-managed organizations run on distributed decisions. That means memory failure is not a side issue.

It creates real operational risk:

- Decisions evaporate.
- Role changes get lost.
- Governance history becomes unclear.
- Commitments become hard to trace.
- New members struggle to understand context.
- Power drifts back toward whoever remembers the most.

### Why they are a strong go-to-market wedge

This market is smaller than general SMB, but much easier to enter first because:

- They already understand the cost of bad governance.
- They value transparency and shared memory.
- They use language Saberra already supports.
- They refer within tight communities.
- There is no dominant incumbent for automatic institutional memory.

## Secondary ICP

# Founder-Led Companies at the “Founder Bottleneck” Stage

**Company size:** 10–50 people

**Trigger pain:** The founder can no longer be in every meeting.

### Their internal conversation

> “Too much is still in my head.”
> 
> 
> “I’m tired of being the company search engine.”
> 
> “People keep asking me things we already decided.”
> 
> “We’re growing, but our systems aren’t keeping up.”
> 

### Why this ICP matters

This segment has acute pain and larger market potential. They may not care about Teal language, but they deeply care about:

- Founder dependency
- Repeated decisions
- Slow onboarding
- Missed commitments
- Knowledge loss when people leave
- Operational clarity without hiring a full ops team

This should be the second wedge after proving the product with self-managed organizations.

# 4. Pricing Model Option

## Recommended Structure

# Workspace-Based Pricing

Saberra does not do per-seat pricing.

The value does not accrue to individual users. It accrues to the organization’s shared memory layer.

Per-seat pricing would:

- Undervalue the product
- Create friction for small teams
- Punish collaboration
- Make adoption feel more expensive as more people benefit

Workspace pricing is cleaner.

---

## Pricing Positioning

Saberra should be positioned as:

> More valuable than a knowledge base.

> 
> More durable than a meeting summary tool.
> 
> Far less expensive than enterprise search.

- Directly against the messy combination of “Guru + Fireflies + founder memory + Notion chaos”

# 8. Notion as the Data Layer

## Current Verdict

# Right Call

Notion is a strong early data layer because it provides:

- Human-readable records
- Fast setup
- Low infrastructure cost
- Easy editing and review
- Familiar interface for modern teams
- Strong adoption in the target market
- Built-in relational database behavior
- A clear path into the Notion ecosystem

### Strategic benefit

Saberra benefits from Notion’s expanding AI platform instead of competing with it.

---

## The Risk

Notion could eventually build an ingestion pipeline.

That risk is real.

But the more likely window is **18–24 months** before Notion directly closes this gap in a way that serves this niche deeply.

### The play

Use the window.

Win customers.

Create case studies.

Build ecosystem trust.

Become the institutional memory layer before Notion, Guru, or meeting-summary tools define the category.
