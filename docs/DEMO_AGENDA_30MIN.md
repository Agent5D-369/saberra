# Amora Living Memory Hub - 30-Minute Team Demo

**Goal:** Show the team that Amora has a functioning institutional brain, that their work is being remembered and structured automatically, and that the governance architecture they are building already works.

**Tone:** Grounded and practical. This is not a product pitch. It is a look inside something that is already running.

---

## Opening Hook (2 min)

Open the admin dashboard. Point to the four animated counters at the top.

Say: "These numbers represent time Sera has already saved us. Every meeting that came into roots@amora.cr has been read, structured, and filed. Every person mentioned has a profile. Every task, decision, and risk has been extracted and is waiting for your review. None of that required anyone to take notes or file a report."

Let the counters animate. Pause. Let the number land.

---

## Segment 1 - The Email Becomes Memory (8 min)

**What to show:** A live meeting email turning into structured records.

1. Pull up a recent Google Meet notification email in the roots@amora.cr inbox (or show the Source Emails database in Notion filtered to the most recent entry).
2. Show the raw email - just a notification with a Drive link.
3. Then show the corresponding Meeting record in Notion - title, date, organizer, participants.
4. Click through to the extracted records: a Decision, a Task, a Risk, a Memory Candidate.
5. Show that the Task has an owner field, a due date, and is linked back to the meeting it came from.

Say: "Sera read that email, checked Google Drive for the transcript, sent it to Claude, and wrote all of this. The meeting happened at 10am. By 10:03 it was in Notion."

**Why this lands:** The team will recognize the meeting. They will see their own words turned into structured records. That is the moment it becomes real.

---

## Segment 2 - The Review Queues (5 min)

**What to show:** The queue section of the dashboard. Click a card to open Notion.

1. Point out the queue cards - Canon Changes, Memory Queue, Decisions, CCOS Ledger.
2. Explain that nothing Sera writes is ever marked as final. Everything is a draft, a candidate, or pending review.
3. Click the Canon Changes card - it opens directly in the Notion database.
4. Show one pending canon change request. Explain what "admin review required" means.
5. Show the CCOS Ledger and point to any tension entries Sera has raised on her own.

Say: "Sera does not decide what becomes policy. She notices when something should be reviewed and raises a tension. The team decides. She just makes sure nothing is forgotten."

**Why this lands:** The team will feel safe. The system is not replacing their judgment - it is making sure their decisions get captured.

---

## Segment 3 - Governance Structure (6 min)

**What to show:** Circles, Roles, and Rick's records.

1. Open the Circles database. Show the Technology Architecture circle and whatever other circles exist.
2. Open the Roles database. Show Lead Steward - Technology Architecture and Living Memory Steward.
3. Open the Role Assignments database. Show Rick's two assignments.
4. Open the Policies database. Show GOV-001 (Living Memory Routing Policy) - the first canon policy in the system.

Say: "This is the CCOS. Circles hold domains. Roles hold accountabilities. Policies hold the rules everyone has agreed to. Sera did not invent this structure - she is living inside it, along with the rest of us."

Optional: Show the Policy Ref formula field on GOV-001, where the policy ID auto-increments (POL-00001, etc.) and Policy Ref shows the governance area prefix (GOV-001).

**Why this lands:** The team will see that the organization's governance structure is not just a document - it is a database with real records, real roles, and real accountability.

---

## Segment 4 - The Knowledge Base (4 min)

**What to show:** The Knowledge Base database and a published article.

1. Open the KB database. Show the published articles about Drive permissions and how Sera works.
2. Point out the Status field (Published), Confidence (High), Audience, and Category.
3. Explain that Sera can propose KB articles from any meeting or email that contains clear how-to knowledge.
4. Show the two-field structure: Summary (2-3 sentences) + Key Points (the actual guidance).

Say: "Every time someone on a call explains how something works, Sera can propose it as a KB article. We review it, publish it, and now every future team member can find that knowledge without asking the same question twice."

**Why this lands:** This speaks directly to onboarding pain and knowledge silos - two things that hurt every growing community.

---

## Segment 5 - The Trust Layer (3 min)

**What to show:** The routing policy and the sensitive review queue.

1. Open GOV-001 in the Policies database. Point to the section on what should and should not route through roots@.
2. Point to the Sensitive Review queue on the dashboard (or open the database).
3. Explain the [lm-exclude] marker: any email with [lm-exclude] in the subject is seen but not filed.

Say: "We decided what Sera is allowed to know. That decision is written down, it is versioned, and it is enforced. Sensitive content goes to a separate admin-only database. The team never sees it in the main workspace. This is not just a feature - it is a governance decision we made together."

**Why this lands:** Trust is the hardest thing to build with AI systems. Showing that you thought about this, wrote it down, and encoded it into the system is more credible than any demo.

---

## Close (2 min)

Return to the dashboard hero numbers.

Say: "Sera has been running since [X date]. In that time she has processed [N] emails, captured [N] meetings, extracted [N] tasks, and saved us an estimated [N] hours of manual memory work. She is not perfect - that is why every record goes through a human before it becomes canon. But she does not forget. She does not lose things. She does not get tired at 11pm when the meeting ends. She just remembers."

Offer to answer questions or walk through any specific database.

---

## Tips

- Keep Notion open in split-screen with the dashboard so you can click queue cards and land directly in Notion.
- If the team asks "can Sera do X?" - the answer is almost always "she can propose it, and you decide."
- If a record looks wrong or embarrassing, that is a feature. Point to it and say "this is why we have review queues."
- Do not show the code. Do not explain Railway. The demo is about what the system does for people, not how it works internally.
